Shared Components, System design and Architecture

Full-stack developer and Architect

I served as the architect for three key projects, including the primary system of the entity, which allows enterprises to submit requests for performing industrial and economic activities using the country's natural resources, while also tracking the status of these requests.

Another major project, CIMA, involved developing a system to collect data from various environmental sources, such as temperature and rainfall changes, deforestation, river and water dam levels, fires, and air pollution. This data was streamed through topics, processed by data models, and displayed on dashboards for analysis.

Additionally, I contributed to projects that enabled interoperation and data exchange with other governmental partners. These included systems that handled citizen information and exposed environmental data to regional entities.

As a developer, I integrated information from a tool called the geo-visor, which displays environmental data on themes such as deforestation, weather patterns in mountainous areas, and water resources over time. I connected this data to Power BI dashboards for better visualization and decision-making.

Furthermore, I facilitated the integration of the governmental single sign-on (SSO) system into several Ministry of Environment systems, allowing users to access services without the need for new credentials. I also diagnosed legacy applications and developed a migration plan to transition them to a modern technology stack, making the systems easier to maintain and evolve.
