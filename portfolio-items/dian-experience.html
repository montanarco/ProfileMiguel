Forms and Architecture

Full-stack developer and Architect

I successfully led the development and delivery of key backend features, including a robust API for secure document storage and retrieval, ensuring that only authorized users could access their documents. Additionally, I enhanced the system's logging capabilities and integrated an online payment solution, allowing seamless transactions. I also built a tracking service to process payments made at physical bank offices, ensuring accurate updates to user accounts.

On the frontend, I played a crucial role in creating user-friendly digital tax forms for the citizens of Colombia, enabling them to report taxes, electronically sign documents, and make payments. I contributed to the development of major forms, such as #110 (Enterprise Income), #210 (General People Income), #350 (Consumption in Bars and Restaurants), #420 (Patrimony), #430 (Fuel), and #435 (Carbon). These efforts helped streamline the digital tax reporting process and were accomplished using Angular for the frontend and Java for backend services.

Carrer Growth

This experience significantly broadened my understanding of the software development lifecycle, particularly in the importance of creating well-structured abstractions and ensuring low coupling to build modular, reusable components. I also gained valuable insights into addressing critical security vulnerabilities, such as DNS spoofing, cross-site scripting, and phishing attacks, enhancing the protection of user data and safeguarding against potential scams.

During my time there, I received training that sharpened my frontend development skills, which I applied to deliver real-world solutions. This hands-on experience allowed me to grow as a full-stack developer, integrating both backend and frontend technologies to build secure, scalable applications.
